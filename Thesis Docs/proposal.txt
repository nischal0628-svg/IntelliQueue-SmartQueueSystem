1. Introduction.
The service industry sectors in Nepal including banking, healthcare, government offices,
and private service centers have been showing growing customer traffic in the recent
years. Nevertheless, most institutions continue to rely on manual, old fashioned
techniques of queueing, which cause lengthy queues, congestions, and
misunderstanding. Lack of a centralized digital system does not allow customers to book
their position in advance and check the real-time queue status, as well as to get the right
waiting-time information (NRB Service Experience Report, 2024).
The process of service delivery is usually slow and unpredictable due to the use of manual
tokens distribution and verbal announcements. Employees find it difficult to handle a huge
number of people, to ensure the accuracy of the tokens, and to address or serve a priority
group, including senior citizens or VIPs (Nepal Public Service Analysis, 2023). This leads
to poor coordination and satisfaction of the customers and service providers (Council,
2024).
As a solution to these issues, this project will suggest IntelliQueue as one of the modern
digital queue and tokens management systems which would combine remote booking of
tokens, live tracking of the queue, priority management, and AI-based waiting-time
prediction. The IntelliQueue mobile application will help customers to add tokens and get
notifications, and track their queue status, whereas the staff will manage operations with
the help of a special web-based dashboard ((MoCIT), 2024). Also, there are new services
that make the check-in processes and updates instant, which facilitate the whole service
experience (Service Innovation Trends, 2024).
The system will help to decrease waiting time, minimize crowding, and increase efficiency
of the provided services by substituting the ancient manual approaches with a new digital
one. The project facilitates more favourable, open, and customer-oriented service settings
in Nepal, which is part of the digital transformation of the country (Unit, 2023).
1.2 Problem scenario.
The banks, hospitals, government offices, and other private centers in Nepal have
outdated manual regimes, which make the process of managing queues stressful ( Nepal
Service Efficiency Report, 2023). In the absence of a centralised digital platform, the
customers will not be able to remotely redeem tokens, monitor queue status, and get
approximate waiting time predictions (Digital Nepal Framework, 2024). Visitors are forced
to stay physically, as a visitor depends on verbal updates, or have to check their status
repeatedly, which leads to frustration and inefficiency.
These problems arise from:
• Dependence on manual processes such as paper tokens or verbal
announcements.
• Lack of real-time information, causing customers to stay onsite for extended
periods.
• Overcrowding and confusion during peak hours.
• Difficulty prioritizing urgent or special-case customers.
• No centralized system, requiring staff to manage queues manually.
Supporting Data:
• Studies show customers prefer digital queue systems for predictability and
reduced waiting (Service Quality Report, 2024).
• Crowding in banks and hospitals remains one of the top complaints among visitors
(Nepal Public Service Analysis, 2023).
• Real-time queue management systems improve service efficiency by up to 40% in
similar environments (QueueTech Research, 2024).
Project as a solution.
The IntelliQueue directly addresses the issues above by offering:
• Remote token booking to reduce physical crowding.
• Real-time queue tracking for transparency.
• Priority queue handling for VIPs, senior citizens, and emergency cases.
• AI-based waiting-time prediction to help customers plan their visit.
• QR-based check-in for fast and contactless arrival confirmation.
• Admin dashboard for efficient queue management.
This digital solution modernizes service operations, enhances customer experience, and
supports Nepal’s progress toward digital transformation.

4. Project risks, threats, and contingency plans.
1.Risk: Real-time synchronization issues between the mobile app, dashboard, and
Firebase.
Threat: Queue updates may become delayed or inaccurate.
Contingency Plan: Use optimized Firestore listeners, well-defined database rules, and
conduct early real-time performance testing. ((MoCIT), 2024)
2.Risk: Deployment challenges on Firebase Hosting or app stores.
Threat: The system may experience downtime or fail to deploy correctly.
Contingency Plan: Perform deployment tests in a staging environment and maintain
backup configuration files.
3. Risk: Low user engagement or difficulty adopting the digital system.
Threat: Users may continue preferring traditional physical queues.
Contingency Plan: Provide a simple, intuitive UI, include onboarding guidance, and gather
feedback to refine usability.
4.Risk: Incorrect data entry by users or staff.
Threat: Mismanaged queues and delays may occur.
Contingency Plan: Implement strong input validation, add confirmation prompts, and
provide clear usage instructions.
5. Risk: Limited experience with certain technologies such as Flutter Web or AI estimation.
Threat: Development progress may slow down.
Contingency Plan: Utilize official documentation, online tutorials, and break tasks into
manageable learning segments.
6.Risk: Internet or Firebase downtime affecting real-time updates.
Threat: Queue information may temporarily fail to refresh.
Contingency Plan: Enable offline support and allow temporary manual queue controls for
administrators.
5. Methodology.
The Extreme Programming (XP) methodology was selected as the most appropriate one
to use in the given project since it is most appropriate when it comes to small groups and
limited development cycles. XP focuses on short iterations, minimal design, and constant
feedback and all these features are helpful in the quick development of features like
queue management, token generation, and notification processing. Its iterative nature
gives the developer the ability to develop the system in bits and test as he goes and
improve on features as discoveries are made as the system develops.
XP can also be more easily adapted to changes, as it deals with real-time elements such
as Firebase and novel technologies such as the use of AI-based efficiency in estimating
waiting-time. XP ensures that the workload of development is never overwhelming and
that development is always made in a steady manner by avoiding large tasks, since they
are hard to manage.
Benefits of Extreme Programming include:
• High-Quality Output: Continuous testing improves reliability and reduces bugs.
• Adaptability: Requirements can be adjusted easily as the system evolves.
• Efficiency: Breaking tasks into small sprints supports steady work completion.
• Risk Reduction: Regular iterations help identify issues early in the process.

10. Conclusion.
This project ends with the coming up with of a IntelliQueue that would enhance efficiency
in the services of the banks, hospitals, government offices and other service centers that
are privately situated in Nepal. The conventional queueing habits are known to generate
a long queue, congestion, and unreliable service provision both to the customers and
employees. The system provides an efficient and convenient solution to these frequent
problems by adding new functionalities like remote token booking, tracking queues in real-
time, priority-based servicing, as well as AI-assisted waiting-time estimation.
The XP approach was in favor of the iterative and adaptable development cycle so that
the system functionality can be continuously enhanced during the project schedule.
Firebase also played an important role as it offered real-time synchronization, safe
authentication, and scalable data management, which guaranteed consistent
performance of the system. The mobile application and the administrative dashboard
were made to be easy to use and user-friendly to aid in enhancing usability among the
service seekers and the members of staff as well.
All in all, this project provides a full and operational digital queue management system,
which increases the efficiency of moving to the next service point, decreases the physical
congestion, and leads to more customer satisfaction. It also tackles the current issues in
service sectors in Nepal as well as reinforcing the overall digital transformation in the
country. This project is a powerful and worthy Final Year Project of the 2025/26 academic
year by providing the technically sound solution, easy to use, and socially impactful one.
